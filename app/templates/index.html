How It Works

Simply add tasks, categorise them (e.g., Work, Study, Personal), and specify estimated durations. The system then tracks your activity, analyses input patterns, and generates real-time performance metrics using dynamic visual charts.

It recommends optimal Pomodoro intervals based on workload and focus levels, intelligently groups similar tasks to reduce cognitive load, and adapts your calendar on-the-fly based on task priority, progress, and available time.

About the Pomodoro Technique

The Pomodoro Technique is a proven time management method introduced by Francesco Cirillo. It encourages users to work with the time they have—rather than against it—by breaking work into intervals, usually 25 minutes long, known as “Pomodoros,” followed by short 5-minute breaks. Every fourth interval is followed by a longer break of 15–30 minutes.

This technique helps maintain sustained concentration and prevent burnout. It's ideal for people who struggle with procrastination or constantly feel overwhelmed by their workload. By chunking tasks into manageable time blocks, users stay mentally fresh and gain a tangible sense of progress.

The AI Productivity Coach integrates Pomodoro scheduling into your daily agenda, ensuring each session is tracked, and even offers reminders and visual timers to help you stay on course.

Implementation Tips
  • Set a timer for 25 minutes – focus on a single task.
  • Take a 5-minute break after each Pomodoro.
  • After four Pomodoros, take a longer break (15–30 minutes).
  • Review completed tasks at the end of the day to identify improvement areas.
  • Use visual feedback from the app to adjust future intervals and task batches.
